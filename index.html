Section 01

What is a Mediation?

A mediation transfers the key content of a source text into another language for a specific reader or listener. You do NOT translate word for word. You select relevant information, adapt the register, and add context where necessary so that your target reader can understand.


In this task, you are writing to Tyler — your American exchange student — explaining key information from a German article about George Floyd's death. Tyler is a teenager, not a teacher: your language should feel like a real email to a friend.

✗ NOT a mediation✓ IS a mediation
A word-for-word translationA purposeful, selective summary in the target language
Copying the structure of the source textOrganising information for the target reader's needs
Ignoring who will read your textAlways writing with a specific addressee and purpose in mind
Translating every detailChoosing what is relevant and leaving out what is not
An essay with formal academic languageThe right register — here: a friendly, semi-formal email
Section 02

Structure of a Mediation Email

1

Opening

Introduce the source text and explain why you are writing. Name the article and its origin.

"I recently read a German article by the bpb about the death of George Floyd…"
2

Main Body

Transfer key information in your own words — organised by topic. Most important points first.

"According to the article, Floyd died on 25 May 2020 after officer Chauvin knelt on his neck for nine minutes."
3

Source Signals

Always make clear when you are referring to the article. Never leave the reader guessing where the information comes from.

"The article also highlights… / The text points out… / According to the article…"
4

Closing

Connect the topic to Tyler's perspective, to All American Boys, or invite a response.

"Do you think things have actually changed since Floyd's death? I'd love to hear your take."
Section 03

Before You Write: 4 Key Questions

1

Who is my addressee?

Tyler is an American teenager. He probably knows about George Floyd and BLM — but not about the German bpb article or the statistical comparison with Germany. Adjust your explanations accordingly.

2

What is my purpose?

To inform Tyler about the key content of the German article. You are not arguing, not persuading — you are transferring information in a clear and reader-friendly way.

3

What does my reader already know?

Tyler is American — he likely knows who Floyd was and about BLM. Focus on what the German article specifically says, especially the statistics and political developments.

4

What is the most important information?

Choose only 4–5 key points. Select: the incident → legal outcome → BLM context → statistics/structural racism → current political situation (Trump/reforms).

Section 04

Useful Sentence Starters

💡 Click any phrase to copy it to your clipboard.

📌 Introducing the Source

  • I recently read a German article by the bpb about…
  • I came across a German article published by the bpb, which…
  • The article I want to tell you about was written by Germany's Federal Agency for Civic Education and deals with…
  • Since we've been discussing All American Boys, I thought you might find this interesting:…

📎 Signalling the Source

  • According to the article, …
  • The text points out that …
  • The article also highlights …
  • As the author explains, …
  • The text further states that …
  • One striking fact mentioned in the article is that …

📊 Presenting Statistics & Facts

  • The article mentions that approximately … people …
  • What struck me most was the statistic that …
  • To put this into perspective, …
  • In comparison to Germany, where only …, the US figure is alarming.
  • Although Black Americans make up only about 13% of the population, they account for …

🔗 Structuring & Linking Ideas

  • In addition to this, the article also mentions that …
  • Another important point is that …
  • What is particularly relevant here is …
  • Furthermore, the text draws attention to …
  • This is closely connected to …
  • Building on this, …

🔚 Closing the Email

  • Reading this article really made me think about …
  • I found this particularly relevant to All American Boys because …
  • Do you think things have actually changed since Floyd's death?
  • I'd love to hear your perspective on this.
  • What's your take on the situation in the US today?
  • Let me know what you think — I'd be really interested in your view.

⚠️ Phrases to Avoid

  • ✗ "It can be argued that…" → too academic
  • ✗ "In conclusion, one can say…" → essay style
  • ✗ "The text is about…" → too vague, no source signal
  • ✗ Starting main body with "I think…" → adds unsupported opinion
  • ✗ Copying sentences from the German text directly

Mediation Knowledge Quiz

10 questions on mediation structure, key facts, and language. Click an answer to proceed.

Question 1 of 10

What is the main purpose of a mediation?

✓ Correct! A mediation selects and transfers relevant content for a specific reader — it is NOT a translation.
✗ Not quite. A mediation is NOT a word-for-word translation. It means selecting, adapting, and transferring key content for a specific reader.
Question 2 of 10

Which phrase best "signals the source" in a mediation email?

✓ Exactly! "According to the article…" clearly signals that the information comes from the source text.
✗ "According to the article…" or "The text points out…" are the correct source-signalling patterns.
Question 3 of 10

How long did Derek Chauvin kneel on George Floyd's neck?

✓ Correct. Chauvin knelt on Floyd's neck for approximately 9 minutes — a key fact for your mediation.
✗ Chauvin knelt on Floyd's neck for approximately 9 minutes. This is an important specific detail.
Question 4 of 10

What percentage of people killed by US police in 2022 were Black Americans?

✓ Correct! Black Americans are ~13% of the population but ~25% of police killings — a key disproportionality statistic.
✗ The key stat: Black Americans are ~13% of the population but ~25% of those killed by police in 2022.
Question 5 of 10

Which register is appropriate for the email to Tyler?

✓ Right! Tyler is your peer — use a friendly, personal tone, not an academic essay style.
✗ Avoid essay language. Tyler is a friend — your email should feel personal and semi-formal.
Question 6 of 10

What is the required word count for this mediation task?

✓ Correct! Aim for 180–220 words. Significantly over or under can affect your mark.
✗ The task requires approximately 180–220 words.
Question 7 of 10

What does "structural racism" refer to?

✓ Correct! Structural racism describes inequalities built into institutions — income gaps, worse schools, five times higher incarceration rates.
✗ Structural racism is about systemic inequalities in institutions — not just individual acts of prejudice.
Question 8 of 10

How many years was Chauvin sentenced to in 2021 at state level?

✓ Correct! 22.5 years — the longest sentence ever given to a police officer in Minnesota.
✗ Chauvin was sentenced to 22.5 years in 2021, the longest sentence ever for a Minnesota police officer.
Question 9 of 10

Which of the following is a common mistake to AVOID in a mediation?

✓ Right! In a mediation, content should stay close to the source. Personal opinions without textual basis go beyond the task.
✗ Adding unsupported personal opinions is a classic mistake. Transfer the article's information — save personal reaction for a brief closing only.
Question 10 of 10

What happened to Biden's police reform programme after Trump took office in 2025?

✓ Correct! Trump abolished diversity programmes in early 2025, threatening Biden-era police reforms.
✗ Trump abolished diversity programmes after taking office in January 2025, putting Biden's 2022 reforms at risk.
